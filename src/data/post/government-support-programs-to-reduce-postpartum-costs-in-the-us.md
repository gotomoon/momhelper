## 1. WIC — Food and Nutrition Support

WIC (Women, Infants, and Children) is a federal nutrition program that provides monthly food benefits, formula, and breastfeeding support to eligible mothers and young children.

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-green-700 mb-3">What WIC Covers</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Monthly food vouchers (fruits, vegetables, grains, dairy, protein)</li>
      <li>Infant formula and baby food</li>
      <li>Breastfeeding support and lactation counseling</li>
      <li>Nutrition education and personalized guidance</li>
      <li>Approximately $47/month for fruits and vegetables (2025)</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white border border-slate-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-slate-700 mb-3">Eligibility & Duration</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Income up to 185% of the federal poverty level</li>
      <li>Non-breastfeeding mothers: up to 6 months postpartum</li>
      <li>Breastfeeding mothers: up to 12 months postpartum</li>
      <li>Infants and children up to age 5</li>
      <li>Total monthly value: typically $150–$300+</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

**How to apply:** Visit [signupwic.com](https://signupwic.com) or call your local WIC office. You can apply before your due date and benefits begin shortly after enrollment.

## 2. Medicaid — 12-Month Postpartum Healthcare Coverage

If you received Medicaid during pregnancy, your coverage now extends for **12 full months after delivery** in 46 states and D.C. (as of 2024). This was made permanent by the Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2023.

<div class="space-y-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-xl border border-slate-200 overflow-hidden">
    <div class="bg-blue-600 px-5 py-3 text-white font-bold text-sm">What the 12-Month Extension Covers</div>
    <div class="p-5 text-sm">
      <ul class="space-y-1 mb-0">
        <li>Postpartum doctor visits and preventive care</li>
        <li>Prescription medications</li>
        <li>Mental health services — including postpartum depression treatment</li>
        <li>Hospital care for complications</li>
        <li>Ongoing chronic condition management</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-xl border border-slate-200 overflow-hidden">
    <div class="bg-teal-600 px-5 py-3 text-white font-bold text-sm">Newborn Coverage — Automatic Enrollment</div>
    <div class="p-5 text-sm">
      <p class="mb-0">Babies born to Medicaid-enrolled mothers are automatically covered from birth through at least age 1. No separate application is required. Verify enrollment with your state Medicaid office after delivery.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

**How to apply:** If you were enrolled in Medicaid during pregnancy, postpartum coverage extends automatically. Check your state's Medicaid office for details: [Medicaid.gov](https://www.medicaid.gov).

## 3. SNAP — Monthly Food Assistance

SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program, formerly food stamps) provides monthly grocery benefits. Postpartum mothers on unpaid leave may newly qualify if household income drops.

<div class="bg-amber-50 border border-amber-200 rounded-lg p-5 my-6">
  <p class="text-sm mb-2"><strong>Important for new mothers:</strong> When your baby is born, your newborn is added to your household size, which can increase your monthly SNAP benefit even if your income stays the same.</p>
  <p class="text-sm mb-0">WIC and SNAP can be used together — they serve different purposes and do not cancel each other out.</p>
</div>

**How to apply:** Visit [USA.gov/food-stamps](https://www.usa.gov/food-stamps) or your state's SNAP office. Eligibility is based on income and household size.

## 4. CHIP — Health Insurance for Your Newborn

CHIP (Children's Health Insurance Program) covers children in families who earn too much to qualify for Medicaid but cannot afford private insurance.

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-blue-50 border border-blue-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 mb-3">CHIP Coverage Includes</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Doctor visits and preventive care</li>
      <li>Immunizations</li>
      <li>Dental and vision coverage</li>
      <li>Hospital care</li>
      <li>Prescription medications</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white border border-slate-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-slate-700 mb-3">Eligibility</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>For children under 19</li>
      <li>Income typically up to 200% of the federal poverty level (varies by state)</li>
      <li>No insurance required (complements employer plans)</li>
      <li>Low or no monthly premium</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

**How to apply:** Through your state Medicaid/CHIP office or at [Medicaid.gov/chip](https://www.medicaid.gov/chip/chip-eligibility-enrollment).

## 5. Marketplace Insurance — Special Enrollment After Birth

If you don't have Medicaid, the birth of your baby triggers a **60-day Special Enrollment Period** to join or change a Marketplace health plan.

<div class="bg-slate-50 rounded-xl border border-slate-200 p-6 my-6">
  <p class="mb-2"><strong>Key advantage:</strong> Coverage starts on the baby's date of birth, regardless of when you enroll — as long as you enroll within 60 days.</p>
  <p class="mb-0">If your household income qualifies, you may receive premium subsidies to reduce your monthly insurance cost. Enroll at <a href="https://www.healthcare.gov">Healthcare.gov</a> or your state marketplace.</p>
</div>

## 6. Free Breast Pump and Lactation Support

Under the Affordable Care Act (ACA), most health insurance plans are required to cover:

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-3">Covered at No Cost</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Double electric breast pump</li>
      <li>Lactation counseling and consultations</li>
      <li>Breastfeeding education</li>
      <li>Breast milk storage supplies</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-slate-700 mb-3">How to Access</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Contact your insurance provider directly</li>
      <li>Ask for a referral from your OB-GYN</li>
      <li>WIC also provides breastfeeding support independently</li>
      <li>Coverage applies to Marketplace and most employer plans</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

Learn more at [Healthcare.gov/coverage/breast-feeding-benefits](https://www.healthcare.gov/coverage/breast-feeding-benefits/).

## 7. Tax Credits and Pre-Tax Accounts

<div class="space-y-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-xl border border-slate-200 overflow-hidden">
    <div class="bg-orange-500 px-5 py-3 text-white font-bold text-sm">Child Tax Credit — Up to $2,000 per child (2024)</div>
    <div class="p-5 text-sm">
      <p class="mb-2">A direct credit against your federal tax bill for each qualifying child under 17. Up to $1,700 is refundable, meaning you may receive it as a refund even if you owe little or no tax.</p>
      <p class="mb-0">Your newborn qualifies as long as they receive a Social Security number before your tax filing deadline. Claim on your annual tax return — no separate application needed.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-xl border border-slate-200 overflow-hidden">
    <div class="bg-teal-600 px-5 py-3 text-white font-bold text-sm">Child and Dependent Care Tax Credit — Up to $2,100</div>
    <div class="p-5 text-sm">
      <p class="mb-2">Covers a percentage of qualifying childcare expenses — daycare, in-home care, preschool, and day camps. For one child, up to $3,000 in expenses qualifies; for two or more children, up to $6,000.</p>
      <p class="mb-0">The credit percentage ranges from 20% to 35% depending on your income. Claim using Form 2441 with your tax return.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-xl border border-slate-200 overflow-hidden">
    <div class="bg-blue-500 px-5 py-3 text-white font-bold text-sm">Dependent Care FSA — Up to $5,000 Pre-Tax</div>
    <div class="p-5 text-sm">
      <p class="mb-2">If your employer offers a Dependent Care Flexible Spending Account, you can set aside up to $5,000 per household per year in pre-tax dollars for childcare. This reduces your taxable income, saving 20%–37% depending on your tax bracket.</p>
      <p class="mb-0">Enroll through your employer's benefits portal. Birth is a qualifying life event that allows mid-year enrollment changes.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-xl border border-slate-200 overflow-hidden">
    <div class="bg-purple-600 px-5 py-3 text-white font-bold text-sm">HSA (Health Savings Account) — Up to $8,750 Pre-Tax (2025)</div>
    <div class="p-5 text-sm">
      <p class="mb-2">If you have a high-deductible health plan, you can use HSA funds tax-free for breast pumps, lactation supplies, postpartum OB visits, baby health expenses, and prescriptions.</p>
      <p class="mb-0">HSAs offer a triple tax advantage: pre-tax contributions, tax-free growth, and tax-free withdrawals for medical expenses. Unused funds roll over year to year.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

## 8. FMLA — Job-Protected Parental Leave

The Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) guarantees eligible employees up to **12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave** for childbirth and bonding.

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-slate-700 mb-3">FMLA Eligibility</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Employed for at least 12 months</li>
      <li>Worked at least 1,250 hours in the past year</li>
      <li>Employer has 50+ employees within 75 miles</li>
      <li>Applies to federal, state, and private employers</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-amber-50 border border-amber-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-amber-700 mb-3">State Paid Leave Programs</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mb-0">Several states offer paid family leave on top of FMLA: California, New York, New Jersey, Washington, Massachusetts, Connecticut, Oregon, Colorado, and others. Check your state's labor department for details.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## 9. Community Health Centers — Low-Cost Postpartum Care

Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs) provide comprehensive postpartum and newborn care on a sliding-scale fee based on your income. Many offer services for free or at very low cost.

Find a health center near you: [findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov](https://findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov)
