## What Is a Postpartum Doula?

A **postpartum doula** is a trained professional who supports mothers and families in the days, weeks, and months after birth. The word "doula" comes from Greek, meaning "a woman who serves." In modern usage, it refers to a non-medical care professional who provides physical, emotional, and practical support during the postpartum period.

Unlike a birth doula (who supports you during labor), a **postpartum doula** arrives after the baby is born and helps the family settle into their new reality.

---

## What Does a Postpartum Doula Do?

A postpartum doula typically provides:

- **Newborn care guidance** — feeding rhythms, sleep coaching, soothing techniques
- **Breastfeeding support** — positioning, latch assistance, pumping guidance
- **Maternal recovery support** — physical comfort measures and rest encouragement
- **Emotional support** — listening, reassurance, and postpartum depression awareness
- **Household support** — light tasks related to the baby and mother
- **Sibling and family adjustment** — helping older children adapt to the new baby

## Why Korean-American Families Prefer Korean Postpartum Care

Many Korean-American mothers grew up with the expectation of Korean-style postpartum recovery — support from family elders, warm nutritional soups, strict rest protocols. When giving birth in the United States, far from family, that support often isn't available.

A **Korean postpartum doula** (산후도우미) bridges this gap by bringing the full Korean postpartum care tradition to your home in the US, including:

- Traditional Korean recovery meals (miyeokguk, seaweed soup, etc.)
- Breast massage and lactation support rooted in Korean practice
- Korean language communication for mothers who are more comfortable in Korean
- Cultural understanding of family dynamics and expectations
