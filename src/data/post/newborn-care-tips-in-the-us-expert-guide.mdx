### A Newborn's Typical Day

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-l-4 border-blue-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 dark:text-blue-400 mb-3">😴 Sleep</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>14–17 hours of sleep per day</li>
      <li>Wakes every 2–4 hours</li>
      <li>Cannot tell day from night (first few weeks)</li>
      <li>Light sleep means frequent waking</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-l-4 border-orange-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 dark:text-orange-400 mb-3">🍼 Feeding</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Feeds every 2–3 hours</li>
      <li>Each session takes 10–45 minutes</li>
      <li>8–12 feedings per day</li>
      <li>More frequent during growth spurts</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-l-4 border-teal-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-teal-700 dark:text-teal-400 mb-3">🚼 Diapers</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>8–12 wet diapers per day</li>
      <li>3–4 dirty diapers per day (more with breastfeeding)</li>
      <li>First few days: dark meconium stool</li>
      <li>Gradually transitions to yellow, soft stool</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-l-4 border-purple-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-purple-700 dark:text-purple-400 mb-3">😢 Crying</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>1–3 hours of crying per day is normal</li>
      <li>Crying is a newborn's only way to communicate</li>
      <li>Evening hours tend to be fussier (colic)</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 2. Safe Holding and Handling

### 3 Basic Holding Positions

<div class="space-y-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-l-4 border-orange-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 dark:text-orange-400 mb-2">🤱 Cradle Hold</h4>
    <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">
      <ol class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
        <li>Support baby's head and neck with one arm</li>
        <li>Support the bottom with the other arm</li>
        <li>Hold baby close to your chest</li>
        <li>Keep baby's face visible</li>
      </ol>
      <div class="text-sm">
        <p class="text-green-600 dark:text-green-400 mb-1"><strong>✅ Best for:</strong> Comfortable, stable hold with eye contact</p>
        <p class="text-red-600 dark:text-red-400 mb-0"><strong>⚠️ Note:</strong> Your arm may tire with extended use</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-l-4 border-teal-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-teal-700 dark:text-teal-400 mb-2">🏈 Football Hold</h4>
    <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">
      <ol class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
        <li>Tuck baby under your arm like a football</li>
        <li>Support the head and neck with your hand</li>
        <li>Baby's body runs along your forearm</li>
        <li>Baby faces up, looking at you</li>
      </ol>
      <div class="text-sm">
        <p class="text-green-600 dark:text-green-400 mb-1"><strong>✅ Best for:</strong> Nursing and burping</p>
        <p class="text-red-600 dark:text-red-400 mb-0"><strong>⚠️ Note:</strong> One hand is always occupied</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-l-4 border-blue-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 dark:text-blue-400 mb-2">💆 Shoulder Hold</h4>
    <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">
      <ol class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
        <li>Hold baby upright against your chest</li>
        <li>Rest baby's head on your shoulder</li>
        <li>One hand supports head and neck</li>
        <li>Other hand supports the bottom</li>
      </ol>
      <div class="text-sm">
        <p class="text-green-600 dark:text-green-400 mb-1"><strong>✅ Best for:</strong> Burping — babies love this one</p>
        <p class="text-red-600 dark:text-red-400 mb-0"><strong>⚠️ Note:</strong> Spit-up may land on your shoulder</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 border border-red-300 dark:border-red-800 rounded-xl p-5 my-6">
  <h4 class="font-bold text-red-700 dark:text-red-400 mb-3">🚫 Never Do These</h4>
  <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Never shake a baby — risk of <strong>Shaken Baby Syndrome (SBS)</strong></li>
    <li>Never hold a baby without supporting the head and neck</li>
    <li>Never toss a baby in the air or play roughly</li>
    <li>Never handle a baby when you are angry or overwhelmed</li>
    <li>Never shake a sleeping baby awake</li>
  </ul>
</div>

### Baby Bath Time

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-blue-50 dark:bg-blue-900/20 border border-blue-200 dark:border-blue-800 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 dark:text-blue-400 mb-3">🧽 First 2 Weeks: Sponge Bath</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mb-2">Avoid immersion baths until the umbilical cord stump falls off.</p>
    <p class="text-xs font-semibold mb-1">Order: Face → Neck → Arms → Legs → Back → Diaper area</p>
    <p class="text-xs text-slate-500 mb-0">Dry each area immediately after washing</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-teal-50 dark:bg-teal-900/20 border border-teal-200 dark:border-teal-800 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-teal-700 dark:text-teal-400 mb-3">🛁 After: Tub Bath Safety Rules</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Water temperature: 97–100°F (test with elbow)</li>
      <li>Water depth: 2–3 inches only</li>
      <li>Never leave baby unattended</li>
      <li>One hand on baby at all times</li>
      <li>Use a non-slip bath mat</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 3. Everything About Feeding

<img src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1492725764893-90b379c2b6e7?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80" alt="Newborn feeding in the U.S. - breastfeeding and formula" class="rounded-xl shadow-lg my-6 w-full object-cover" style="max-height: 400px;" />

### Breastfeeding: Getting the Latch Right

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-green-50 dark:bg-green-900/20 border border-green-200 dark:border-green-800 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-green-700 dark:text-green-400 mb-3">✅ Signs of a Good Latch</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Baby's mouth opens wide</li>
      <li>Baby takes in the areola, not just the nipple</li>
      <li>Baby's chin touches the breast</li>
      <li>Lips are flanged outward</li>
      <li>Rhythmic suck-swallow-breathe pattern</li>
      <li>No pain for mom</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 border border-red-200 dark:border-red-800 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-red-700 dark:text-red-400 mb-3">❌ Signs of a Poor Latch</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Baby is only latched on the nipple</li>
      <li>Clicking or smacking sounds</li>
      <li>Baby's cheeks are sucked in</li>
      <li>Mom feels pain</li>
      <li>Nipple looks flattened after feeding</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 4. Sleep Safety and Building a Routine

<img src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1537368910025-700350fe46c7?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80" alt="Safe newborn sleep in the U.S. — SIDS prevention" class="rounded-xl shadow-lg my-6 w-full object-cover" style="max-height: 400px;" />

### SIDS Prevention — American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) Guidelines

<div class="bg-green-50 dark:bg-green-900/20 border border-green-200 dark:border-green-800 rounded-xl p-6 my-6">
  <h4 class="font-bold text-green-700 dark:text-green-400 mb-4">✅ Safe Sleep Rules You Must Follow</h4>
  <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-2">
    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-green-500 font-bold">✓</span> Always place baby on their <strong>back to sleep</strong></div>
    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-green-500 font-bold">✓</span> Use a <strong>firm, flat mattress</strong></div>
    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-green-500 font-bold">✓</span> <strong>Nothing else in the crib</strong> — no pillows, blankets, or toys</div>
    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-green-500 font-bold">✓</span> Keep room temperature at <strong>68–72°F (20–22°C)</strong></div>
    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-green-500 font-bold">✓</span> Baby sleeps in <strong>own crib</strong> in your room (not your bed)</div>
    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-green-500 font-bold">✓</span> <strong>Smoke-free environment</strong></div>
  </div>
</div>

## 5. Diaper Changes and Hygiene

### Step-by-Step Diaper Change

<div class="bg-slate-50 dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-xl p-5 my-6">
  <div class="space-y-3">
    <div class="flex gap-3 items-start text-sm"><span class="bg-orange-500 text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center text-xs font-bold flex-shrink-0">1</span><div><strong>Prepare:</strong> Have diapers, wipes, cream, and a clean outfit within reach before you start — never leave baby unattended</div></div>
    <div class="flex gap-3 items-start text-sm"><span class="bg-orange-500 text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center text-xs font-bold flex-shrink-0">2</span><div><strong>Remove:</strong> Unfasten the diaper tabs, fold the front down, and use the clean part to wipe the baby</div></div>
    <div class="flex gap-3 items-start text-sm"><span class="bg-orange-500 text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center text-xs font-bold flex-shrink-0">3</span><div><strong>Wipe:</strong> Girls: always <strong>front to back</strong> (prevents UTI) · Boys: clean all skin folds thoroughly</div></div>
    <div class="flex gap-3 items-start text-sm"><span class="bg-orange-500 text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center text-xs font-bold flex-shrink-0">4</span><div><strong>Dry:</strong> Remove all moisture completely · Air-dry briefly · Apply diaper cream if needed</div></div>
    <div class="flex gap-3 items-start text-sm"><span class="bg-orange-500 text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center text-xs font-bold flex-shrink-0">5</span><div><strong>New Diaper:</strong> Position below the navel · Two fingers should fit between the diaper and belly</div></div>
  </div>
</div>

### Umbilical Cord Stump Care

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-4">
  <div class="bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 border border-amber-200 dark:border-amber-800 rounded-lg p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-amber-700 dark:text-amber-400 mb-2 text-sm">Before It Falls Off (Weeks 1–3)</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Keep it dry at all times</li>
      <li>Fold diaper below the stump</li>
      <li>Stick to sponge baths</li>
      <li>See doctor if it turns red or swollen</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-green-50 dark:bg-green-900/20 border border-green-200 dark:border-green-800 rounded-lg p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-green-700 dark:text-green-400 mb-2 text-sm">After It Falls Off</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Keep the area clean and dry</li>
      <li>Some discharge for a few days is normal</li>
      <li>See doctor if there is odor or pus</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 6. Understanding and Soothing Crying

### Types of Cries

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-3 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-4 border-l-4 border-orange-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-sm mb-1">🍼 Hunger</h4>
    <p class="text-xs text-slate-500 mb-0">Starts low and rhythmic → builds louder · Accompanied by rooting reflex · Hands move toward mouth</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-4 border-l-4 border-blue-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-sm mb-1">😴 Tiredness</h4>
    <p class="text-xs text-slate-500 mb-0">Whining · Yawning · Eye rubbing · Looking away</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-4 border-l-4 border-teal-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-sm mb-1">🚼 Dirty Diaper</h4>
    <p class="text-xs text-slate-500 mb-0">Sudden onset · Medium pitch · Usually brief</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-4 border-l-4 border-red-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-sm mb-1">😣 Gas / Colic</h4>
    <p class="text-xs text-slate-500 mb-0">Sharp, piercing cry · Reddened face · Legs pulled toward belly · Worse in evenings</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-4 border-l-4 border-purple-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-sm mb-1">🌀 Overstimulation</h4>
    <p class="text-xs text-slate-500 mb-0">Escalating cry · Avoiding eye contact · Body stiffening</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-4 border-l-4 border-slate-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-sm mb-1">🤒 Pain / Discomfort</h4>
    <p class="text-xs text-slate-500 mb-0">Sudden and loud · Won't stop · Sounds different than usual · May have other symptoms (fever, vomiting)</p>
  </div>
</div>

### The 5 S's Method (Dr. Harvey Karp)

<div class="bg-teal-50 dark:bg-teal-900/20 border border-teal-200 dark:border-teal-800 rounded-xl p-6 my-6">
  <h4 class="font-bold text-teal-700 dark:text-teal-400 mb-4">5-Step Newborn Soothing Method</h4>
  <div class="space-y-3">
    <div class="flex gap-3 items-start"><span class="bg-teal-500 text-white font-bold text-sm rounded-lg px-2 py-1 flex-shrink-0">S1</span><div class="text-sm"><strong>Swaddle</strong> — Wrap baby snugly with arms at their sides to recreate the feeling of the womb.</div></div>
    <div class="flex gap-3 items-start"><span class="bg-teal-500 text-white font-bold text-sm rounded-lg px-2 py-1 flex-shrink-0">S2</span><div class="text-sm"><strong>Side or Stomach Position</strong> — Hold baby on their side or stomach while awake. Always put them on their back to sleep.</div></div>
    <div class="flex gap-3 items-start"><span class="bg-teal-500 text-white font-bold text-sm rounded-lg px-2 py-1 flex-shrink-0">S3</span><div class="text-sm"><strong>Shush</strong> — Make a loud "shhhh" sound close to baby's ear, or use a white noise app. Keep it going.</div></div>
    <div class="flex gap-3 items-start"><span class="bg-teal-500 text-white font-bold text-sm rounded-lg px-2 py-1 flex-shrink-0">S4</span><div class="text-sm"><strong>Swing</strong> — Gently rock baby side to side while fully supporting the head. Keep movements smooth, not vigorous.</div></div>
    <div class="flex gap-3 items-start"><span class="bg-teal-500 text-white font-bold text-sm rounded-lg px-2 py-1 flex-shrink-0">S5</span><div class="text-sm"><strong>Suck</strong> — Offer a pacifier or let baby suck on a finger. Sucking itself has a calming effect.</div></div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 border-l-4 border-amber-400 rounded-r-lg p-4 my-4">
  <p class="font-bold text-amber-800 dark:text-amber-300 mb-1">What Is Colic?</p>
  <p class="text-sm mb-0">Crying for 3+ hours per day, 3+ days per week, for 3+ weeks. Common between 2 weeks and 4 months. Harmless but exhausting. If you need a break, place baby safely in the crib and step outside for a moment. It's okay to ask for help.</p>
</div>

## 7. Health Checks and Warning Signs

### Normal Newborn Symptoms (No Need to Worry)

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-slate-50 dark:bg-slate-700 rounded-lg p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-sm mb-2">🧴 Skin and Head</h4>
    <ul class="text-xs space-y-1 mb-0 text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-300">
      <li>Red or purple at birth → peeling skin after a few days</li>
      <li>Tiny white bumps (milia) · red patches (stork bites)</li>
      <li>Cone-shaped head → rounds out within days</li>
      <li>Soft spot on top (fontanelle) is normal</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-slate-50 dark:bg-slate-700 rounded-lg p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-sm mb-2">👁️ Eyes, Belly, and Stool</h4>
    <ul class="text-xs space-y-1 mb-0 text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-300">
      <li>Red eyes · slightly crossed · eye discharge</li>
      <li>Slightly protruding belly · gurgling sounds</li>
      <li>Green stool · seedy texture · color changes daily</li>
      <li>Frequent burping and gas</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

### Emergency Warning Signs

<div class="bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 border border-red-300 dark:border-red-800 rounded-xl p-6 my-6">
  <h4 class="font-bold text-red-700 dark:text-red-400 mb-4 text-lg">🚨 Call Your Doctor Immediately For:</h4>
  <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-2">
    <div class="flex items-start gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500 flex-shrink-0">●</span> Fever of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher — in babies under 3 months</div>
    <div class="flex items-start gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500 flex-shrink-0">●</span> Breathing difficulty — too fast, too slow, or grunting</div>
    <div class="flex items-start gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500 flex-shrink-0">●</span> Bluish color around lips or face (cyanosis)</div>
    <div class="flex items-start gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500 flex-shrink-0">●</span> Seizures or tremors</div>
    <div class="flex items-start gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500 flex-shrink-0">●</span> Signs of dehydration — no wet diaper for 8+ hours</div>
    <div class="flex items-start gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500 flex-shrink-0">●</span> Lethargic or very difficult to wake</div>
    <div class="flex items-start gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500 flex-shrink-0">●</span> Inconsolable crying that won't stop</div>
    <div class="flex items-start gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500 flex-shrink-0">●</span> Forceful, projectile vomiting</div>
    <div class="flex items-start gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500 flex-shrink-0">●</span> Hard, distended belly</div>
    <div class="flex items-start gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500 flex-shrink-0">●</span> Redness or pus around the umbilical stump</div>
  </div>
</div>

    <div class="space-y-2 text-sm">
      <div><p class="font-semibold text-xs text-slate-500 mb-1">Physical</p><p class="mb-0">Holds head at 45° · Hands begin to open · Smoother movements</p></div>

    <div class="space-y-2 text-sm">
      <div><p class="font-semibold text-xs text-slate-500 mb-1">Physical</p><p class="mb-0">Pushes up on arms during tummy time · Stronger kicks</p></div>
      <div><p class="font-semibold text-xs text-slate-500 mb-1">Senses</p><p class="mb-0">Studies own hands · Reaches for toys · Better distance vision</p></div>
