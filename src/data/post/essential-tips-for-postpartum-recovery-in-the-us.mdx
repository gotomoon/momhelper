## 1. Understanding What Happens to Your Body After Birth

### The 6-Week Postpartum Period

Medically, the first six weeks after birth are called the **"postpartum period"** — a time of remarkable change and recovery in your body.

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-l-4 border-orange-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 dark:text-orange-400 mb-2">🫁 Uterine Contractions</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Your uterus shrinks from watermelon-size back to normal over 6 weeks</li>
      <li>You may feel <strong>afterpains</strong> during this process</li>
      <li>Contractions feel stronger during breastfeeding</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-l-4 border-orange-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 dark:text-orange-400 mb-2">🩸 Lochia (Postpartum Bleeding)</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Vaginal discharge continues for 2–6 weeks after birth</li>
      <li>Starts bright red → turns brown → then yellowish</li>
      <li>See your doctor if it suddenly increases or smells bad</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-l-4 border-teal-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-teal-700 dark:text-teal-400 mb-2">⚡ Hormone Shifts</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Pregnancy hormones drop sharply; nursing hormones rise</li>
      <li>Can cause mood swings, increased sweating, and hair loss</li>
      <li>Usually temporary; severe cases may need professional support</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-l-4 border-teal-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-teal-700 dark:text-teal-400 mb-2">🦴 Pelvic Floor Recovery</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Stretched pelvic floor muscles take months to recover</li>
      <li>Urinary leakage and pelvic discomfort are common</li>
      <li><strong>Kegel exercises</strong> support gradual recovery</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 2. Rest Is Your Best Medicine

<img src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1541781774459-bb2af2f05b55?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80" alt="Resting for postpartum recovery" class="rounded-xl shadow-lg my-6 w-full object-cover" style="max-height: 400px;" />

### Sleep Is Healing

The single most important tool for **postpartum recovery** is sleep. While you sleep, your body repairs damaged tissue, rebalances hormones, and rebuilds your immune system.

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-3 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-blue-50 dark:bg-blue-900/20 rounded-lg p-4 text-center">
    <div class="text-3xl mb-2">👶</div>
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 dark:text-blue-400 mb-1 text-sm">Sleep When Baby Sleeps</h4>
    <p class="text-xs mb-0">Follow the golden rule: "When baby sleeps, mom sleeps." The dishes and laundry can wait.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-blue-50 dark:bg-blue-900/20 rounded-lg p-4 text-center">
    <div class="text-3xl mb-2">🌙</div>
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 dark:text-blue-400 mb-1 text-sm">Share Night Duties</h4>
    <p class="text-xs mb-0">Your partner can handle diaper changes and bring the baby for feeding so you can go right back to sleep.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-blue-50 dark:bg-blue-900/20 rounded-lg p-4 text-center">
    <div class="text-3xl mb-2">🔇</div>
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 dark:text-blue-400 mb-1 text-sm">Optimize Your Sleep Space</h4>
    <p class="text-xs mb-0">Keep the room dark and cool. Use white noise. Turn off phone notifications to eliminate distractions.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## 3. Postpartum Nutrition: Eating for Recovery

<img src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1498837167922-ddd27525d352?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80" alt="Healthy postpartum nutrition and meals" class="rounded-xl shadow-lg my-6 w-full object-cover" style="max-height: 400px;" />

### The Science Behind Korean Postpartum Food

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-t-4 border-orange-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 dark:text-orange-400 mb-3">🍲 Seaweed Soup (Miyeok-guk)</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Rich in iodine → supports thyroid recovery</li>
      <li>High fiber → prevents constipation</li>
      <li>Calcium-rich → supports bone health</li>
      <li>Warm broth → keeps you hydrated</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-t-4 border-orange-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 dark:text-orange-400 mb-3">🍗 Protein-Centered Meals</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Chicken, beef, fish, tofu, eggs</li>
      <li>Essential for tissue repair and milk production</li>
      <li>Aim for a palm-size portion of protein at every meal</li>
      <li>Pair with iron-rich foods</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

### What to Eat vs. What to Avoid

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-green-50 dark:bg-green-900/20 border border-green-200 dark:border-green-800 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-green-700 dark:text-green-400 mb-3">✅ Recommended Foods</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-2 mb-0">
      <li>🌿 <strong>Seaweed soup</strong> — iodine, calcium, fiber</li>
      <li>🥩 <strong>Iron-rich foods</strong> — spinach, beef, black beans</li>
      <li>🥚 <strong>Protein</strong> — hard-boiled eggs, Greek yogurt</li>
      <li>🫐 <strong>Overnight oats</strong> — convenient iron and fiber boost</li>
      <li>💧 <strong>8–10 glasses of water daily</strong> — especially while nursing</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 border border-red-200 dark:border-red-800 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-red-700 dark:text-red-400 mb-3">❌ Foods to Avoid</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-2 mb-0">
      <li>☕ <strong>Caffeine</strong> — limit to 1–2 cups/day (passes into breast milk)</li>
      <li>🍺 <strong>Alcohol</strong> — avoid completely for safest recovery</li>
      <li>🍔 <strong>Processed & junk food</strong> — promotes inflammation, hinders healing</li>
      <li>🧊 <strong>Cold foods & drinks</strong> — can impair digestion and recovery</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 4. C-Section Postpartum Recovery

<div class="bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 border-l-4 border-amber-500 rounded-r-xl p-5 my-6">
  <p class="font-bold text-amber-800 dark:text-amber-300 mb-1">⚠️ A C-section is major surgery</p>
  <p class="text-sm mb-0">Because it involves abdominal surgery, recovery takes longer than a vaginal birth and requires special care. Take your prescribed pain medication on schedule — pushing through pain slows healing.</p>
</div>

### C-Section Recovery Timeline

<div class="space-y-3 my-6">
  <div class="flex gap-4 items-start bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg p-4 shadow-sm">
    <div class="bg-red-100 dark:bg-red-900/40 text-red-700 dark:text-red-300 font-bold text-sm rounded-full px-3 py-1 whitespace-nowrap">Weeks 1–2</div>
    <div>
      <p class="font-semibold mb-1 text-sm">Bed Rest Priority</p>
      <p class="text-sm mb-0 text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-400">Light indoor activity only · Focus solely on baby care and feeding · Gently clean incision once daily, dry completely · Avoid tight clothing</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex gap-4 items-start bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg p-4 shadow-sm">
    <div class="bg-orange-100 dark:bg-orange-900/40 text-orange-700 dark:text-orange-300 font-bold text-sm rounded-full px-3 py-1 whitespace-nowrap">Weeks 3–4</div>
    <div>
      <p class="font-semibold mb-1 text-sm">Gradual Activity Increase</p>
      <p class="text-sm mb-0 text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-400">Short walks okay · Still no heavy lifting · Roll to your side when getting out of bed · Use a pillow when coughing or laughing</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex gap-4 items-start bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg p-4 shadow-sm">
    <div class="bg-green-100 dark:bg-green-900/40 text-green-700 dark:text-green-300 font-bold text-sm rounded-full px-3 py-1 whitespace-nowrap">Weeks 6–8</div>
    <div>
      <p class="font-semibold mb-1 text-sm">Assessment and Return to Normal</p>
      <p class="text-sm mb-0 text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-400">6-week checkup to assess recovery · Resume driving (with doctor approval) · Light exercise can begin</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 border border-red-200 dark:border-red-800 rounded-lg p-5 my-6">
  <h4 class="font-bold text-red-700 dark:text-red-400 mb-3">🚨 Contact Your Doctor Immediately If You Notice:</h4>
  <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Foul-smelling discharge from the incision</li>
    <li>Fever of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher</li>
    <li>Feeling like the incision is opening</li>
    <li>Severe pain not controlled by prescribed medication</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## 5. Emotional Recovery and Preventing Postpartum Depression

<img src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1518611012118-696072aa579a?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80" alt="Emotional well-being and postpartum mental health" class="rounded-xl shadow-lg my-6 w-full object-cover" style="max-height: 400px;" />

### Baby Blues vs. Postpartum Depression

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-blue-50 dark:bg-blue-900/20 border border-blue-200 dark:border-blue-800 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 dark:text-blue-400 mb-3">😢 Baby Blues</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-3">
      <li>Affects <strong>70–80%</strong> of new mothers</li>
      <li>Typically starts 2–3 days after birth</li>
      <li>Resolves naturally <strong>within 2 weeks</strong></li>
      <li>No special treatment needed</li>
    </ul>
    <p class="text-xs text-blue-600 dark:text-blue-400 mb-0 font-semibold">Coping: Rest · Talk to someone · Take short walks</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-purple-50 dark:bg-purple-900/20 border border-purple-200 dark:border-purple-800 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-purple-700 dark:text-purple-400 mb-3">🆘 Postpartum Depression (PPD)</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-3">
      <li>Lasts more than 2 weeks</li>
      <li>Interferes with daily life</li>
      <li>Difficulty bonding with baby</li>
      <li><strong>Professional help is essential</strong></li>
    </ul>
    <p class="text-xs text-purple-600 dark:text-purple-400 mb-0 font-semibold">Treatment: Therapy · Support groups · Medication if needed</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="bg-purple-50 dark:bg-purple-900/20 border-l-4 border-purple-500 rounded-r-xl p-5 my-6">
  <p class="font-bold text-purple-800 dark:text-purple-300 mb-2">Seek help immediately if any of these symptoms last more than 2 weeks:</p>
  <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Unable to care for yourself or your baby</li>
    <li>Thoughts of harming yourself or your baby</li>
    <li>Extreme anxiety or panic attacks</li>
    <li>Persistent sadness and hopelessness</li>
  </ul>
</div>

### Strategies for Emotional Recovery

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-4 border-l-4 border-orange-300">
    <h4 class="font-bold mb-2 text-sm">🪞 Self-Care</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Even 10 minutes of alone time helps</li>
      <li>Take a warm shower or bath</li>
      <li>Listen to music you love</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 rounded-lg shadow-sm p-4 border-l-4 border-orange-300">
    <h4 class="font-bold mb-2 text-sm">🤝 Stay Connected</h4>

      <li>Don't isolate yourself</li>

## 6. Pelvic Floor Strengthening and Kegel Exercises

<img src="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1544367567-0f2fcb009e0b?auto=format&fit=crop&w=1200&q=80" alt="Postpartum exercise - yoga and stretching" class="rounded-xl shadow-lg my-6 w-full object-cover" style="max-height: 400px;" />

Your **pelvic floor muscles** support the uterus, bladder, and bowel. Pregnancy and childbirth can weaken them, leading to urinary leakage, pelvic discomfort, and other issues.

### How to Do Kegel Exercises Correctly

<div class="bg-teal-50 dark:bg-teal-900/20 border border-teal-200 dark:border-teal-800 rounded-lg p-5 my-6">
  <h4 class="font-bold text-teal-700 dark:text-teal-400 mb-4">5-Step Kegel Exercise</h4>
  <ol class="space-y-2 mb-4">
    <li class="flex gap-3 text-sm"><span class="bg-teal-500 text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center text-xs font-bold flex-shrink-0">1</span><span>Squeeze the muscles you use to stop urinating</span></li>
    <li class="flex gap-3 text-sm"><span class="bg-teal-500 text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center text-xs font-bold flex-shrink-0">2</span><span>Hold the contraction for 5 seconds</span></li>
    <li class="flex gap-3 text-sm"><span class="bg-teal-500 text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center text-xs font-bold flex-shrink-0">3</span><span>Relax for 5 seconds</span></li>
    <li class="flex gap-3 text-sm"><span class="bg-teal-500 text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center text-xs font-bold flex-shrink-0">4</span><span>Repeat 10 times</span></li>
    <li class="flex gap-3 text-sm"><span class="bg-teal-500 text-white rounded-full w-6 h-6 flex items-center justify-center text-xs font-bold flex-shrink-0">5</span><span>Do 3 sets per day</span></li>
  </ol>
  <p class="text-xs text-teal-700 dark:text-teal-400 mb-0"><strong>When to start:</strong> Vaginal birth — you can begin right away · C-section — wait a few days after delivery</p>
</div>

### Postpartum Exercise Guide

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-green-50 dark:bg-green-900/20 rounded-lg p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-green-700 dark:text-green-400 mb-2 text-sm">✅ Recommended (First 6 Weeks)</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Kegel exercises (pelvic floor)</li>
      <li>Diaphragmatic breathing</li>
      <li>Gentle stretching</li>
      <li>Slow walking (10–15 min)</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 rounded-lg p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-red-700 dark:text-red-400 mb-2 text-sm">❌ Avoid Until After Week 6</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Crunches and planks (increase abdominal pressure)</li>
      <li>High-intensity interval training (HIIT)</li>
      <li>Jumping or running</li>
      <li>Heavy weightlifting</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 7. Breastfeeding and Postpartum Recovery

### How Breastfeeding Affects Recovery

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-green-50 dark:bg-green-900/20 border border-green-200 dark:border-green-800 rounded-lg p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-green-700 dark:text-green-400 mb-2 text-sm">Benefits</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Stimulates uterine contractions → reduces postpartum bleeding</li>
      <li>Supports weight loss (burns about 500 calories/day)</li>
      <li>Lowers risk of postpartum depression</li>
      <li>Builds mother-baby bonding</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-amber-50 dark:bg-amber-900/20 border border-amber-200 dark:border-amber-800 rounded-lg p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-amber-700 dark:text-amber-400 mb-2 text-sm">Things to Keep in Mind</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>You need more calories than usual</li>
      <li>Increase your water intake</li>
      <li>Calcium loss is possible (supplement if needed)</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

### Common Breastfeeding Issues and How to Handle Them

<div class="space-y-3 my-4">
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 border border-slate-200 dark:border-slate-700 rounded-lg p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-sm mb-1">🤱 Nipple Pain</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mb-0 text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-400">Correct latch is key · Rub breast milk on nipples after feeding and let air dry · Use lanolin cream · See a lactation consultant for persistent pain</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white dark:bg-slate-800 border border-slate-200 dark:border-slate-700 rounded-lg p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-sm mb-1">🔴 Breast Engorgement</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mb-0 text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-400">Feed frequently · Apply warm compress before feeding, cold compress after · Gentle massage · Hand express or pump if needed</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 border border-red-200 dark:border-red-800 rounded-lg p-4">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-sm mb-1 text-red-700 dark:text-red-400">🚨 Mastitis (See Your Doctor Right Away)</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mb-0 text-slate-600 dark:text-slate-400">Fever + red, hot, painful area in breast → may need antibiotics · Continue nursing if possible</p>
  </div>
</div>

## 9. Warning Signs: When to Call Your Doctor

<div class="bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 border border-red-300 dark:border-red-800 rounded-xl p-6 my-6">
  <h4 class="font-bold text-red-700 dark:text-red-400 mb-4 text-lg">🚨 Contact Your Doctor Immediately For:</h4>
  <div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-2">
    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500">●</span> Severe abdominal or pelvic pain</div>
    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500">●</span> Foul-smelling vaginal discharge</div>
    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 text-sm"><span class="text-red-500">●</span> Fever of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher</div>
