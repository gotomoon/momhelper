## 1. Hospital Birth Experience

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-blue-50 border border-blue-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 mb-3">Korea</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Vaginal birth: typically 2-3 days in hospital</li>
      <li>C-section: often 5-7 days</li>
      <li>Postpartum-focused education and repeated feeding guidance</li>
      <li>Family involvement and longer visiting culture</li>
      <li>Discharge often followed by postpartum center or family care</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-orange-50 border border-orange-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-3">United States</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Vaginal birth: often 24-48 hours</li>
      <li>C-section: usually 2-4 days</li>
      <li>Essential guidance, but less postpartum specialization</li>
      <li>Visitor limits are common</li>
      <li>Most families go directly home and start care immediately</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 2. Recovery Timeline and Activity Level

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg p-5 shadow-sm border-l-4 border-blue-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 mb-3">Korean Pattern (Samchilil / 21 days)</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Week 1: near-complete rest</li>
      <li>Week 2: light movement indoors</li>
      <li>Week 3: gradual household activity</li>
      <li>After Week 4: activity rises slowly</li>
      <li>Strong emphasis on avoiding overexertion early</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg p-5 shadow-sm border-l-4 border-orange-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-3">U.S. Pattern (6-week medical check)</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Week 1: recovery begins at home</li>
      <li>Week 2: many moms start solo daytime baby care</li>
      <li>Weeks 3-6: gradually return to normal errands/routine</li>
      <li>After 6 weeks: many resume broader activities/work planning</li>
      <li>Faster functional recovery is commonly expected</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 3. Diet and Nutrition

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 mb-3">Korean Postpartum Nutrition</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Warm, digestible, nutrient-dense meals</li>
      <li>Miyeok-guk (seaweed soup) as a staple</li>
      <li>Structured meal support from family/helper</li>
      <li>Traditionally avoids cold foods early postpartum</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-3">Typical U.S. Nutrition Approach</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Balanced calories, hydration, convenience</li>
      <li>Home meals + takeout/frozen options</li>
      <li>No strict postpartum menu in most settings</li>
      <li>Flexible based on individual preference</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 4. Baby Care and Breastfeeding Culture

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-teal-50 border border-teal-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-teal-700 mb-3">Korea</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Mother's recovery is prioritized</li>
      <li>Helpers/family often share newborn workload</li>
      <li>Mixed feeding is commonly accepted</li>
      <li>More room for maternal rest and sleep</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-purple-50 border border-purple-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-purple-700 mb-3">United States</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Rooming-in and direct parent care from day one</li>
      <li>Higher pressure toward exclusive breastfeeding</li>
      <li>Parents learn by doing quickly</li>
      <li>Can increase fatigue without extra support</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 5. Emotional Support and Social Pressure

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border border-slate-200">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 mb-3">Korean Context</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Stronger community/family caregiving norms</li>
      <li>More visible postpartum rituals and support behaviors</li>
      <li>Recovery is socially validated</li>
      <li>But some expectations can also feel restrictive</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border border-slate-200">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-3">U.S. Context</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Higher independence expectation</li>
      <li>Shorter leave and earlier return pressure for many moms</li>
      <li>Better formal mental-health screening access</li>
      <li>But isolation can be more common</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 6. Cost Comparison (High-Level)

<div class="bg-amber-50 border border-amber-200 rounded-lg p-5 my-6">
  <p class="text-sm mb-2"><strong>Korea:</strong> childbirth and postpartum support are generally more accessible through insurance/social structures.</p>
  <p class="text-sm mb-2"><strong>United States:</strong> costs vary widely by insurance, state, city, and care model; private postpartum support can be expensive.</p>
  <p class="text-sm mb-0"><strong>Planning Tip:</strong> build a postpartum budget before delivery (hospital out-of-pocket + 2 to 4 weeks of support + backup care).</p>
</div>

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg border border-slate-200 p-5 text-sm">
    <h4 class="font-bold mb-2">U.S. Cost Factors</h4>
    <ul class="space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Insurance type and deductible</li>
      <li>City/region labor rates</li>
      <li>Daytime vs overnight care</li>
      <li>Total weeks of support</li>
      <li>Agency vs direct hire model</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg border border-slate-200 p-5 text-sm">
    <h4 class="font-bold mb-2">Support Options to Mix</h4>
    <ul class="space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Professional helper for first 2 weeks</li>
      <li>Part-time support in weeks 3-4</li>
      <li>Partner leave scheduling</li>
      <li>Meal prep + community help</li>
      <li>Mental-health check-ins</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 7. Pros and Cons by System

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-green-700 mb-2">Korean-Style Strengths</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Better early rest and nutrition structure</li>
      <li>Shared caregiving lowers immediate stress</li>
      <li>Step-by-step learning support</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-red-50 border border-red-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-red-700 mb-2">Korean-Style Tradeoffs</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Can feel expensive or restrictive</li>
      <li>Possible dependence on external support</li>
      <li>Partner involvement may be reduced in some setups</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-green-700 mb-2">U.S.-Style Strengths</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Fast practical parenting adaptation</li>
      <li>Strong partner-family teamwork opportunity</li>
      <li>Higher flexibility and personal choice</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-red-50 border border-red-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-red-700 mb-2">U.S.-Style Tradeoffs</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Insufficient rest is common</li>
      <li>Higher risk of isolation and burnout</li>
      <li>Paid support can be costly</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 8. Practical Plan for New Moms in the U.S.

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-2">Phase 1 (Weeks 0-2)</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Protect maternal sleep and pain recovery</li>
      <li>Use concentrated helper/family support</li>
      <li>Meal and hydration routine first</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-2">Phase 2 (Weeks 3-4)</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Shift to part-time support</li>
      <li>Build sustainable baby-care rhythm</li>
      <li>Keep one protected rest block daily</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-2">Phase 3 (After Week 4)</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Increase independence gradually</li>
      <li>Track mood, sleep, and physical symptoms</li>
      <li>Maintain backup support plan</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-2">Communication Rule</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Set boundaries early with relatives/visitors</li>
      <li>Align partner responsibilities in writing</li>
      <li>Ask for help before burnout starts</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
