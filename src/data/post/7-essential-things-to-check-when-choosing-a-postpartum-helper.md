<div class="bg-orange-50 border border-orange-200 rounded-xl p-6 my-8">
  <p class="text-lg font-semibold text-orange-800 mb-3">A good helper can protect your recovery. A bad one can make it harder.</p>
  <p class="mb-0">Because postpartum support happens inside your home, choice matters. This checklist organizes the most important screening points before you hire a helper, whether through an agency or direct referral.</p>
</div>

## At a Glance

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-3 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-2">What to Verify</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mb-0">Experience, certifications, communication, professionalism, contract terms, and safety checks.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-2">What to Avoid</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mb-0">Vague answers, missing references, poor boundaries, refusal to use a contract, and hidden costs.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm border border-slate-200 p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-2">Best First Step</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mb-0">Interview at least 2 to 3 candidates and compare them with the same checklist.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## 1. Experience and Certifications

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-blue-50 border border-blue-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 mb-3">Experience to look for</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>At least 1 year of newborn or postpartum care experience</li>
      <li>Experience with different birth and family situations</li>
      <li>Confidence handling feeding, bathing, sleep, and maternal recovery support</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-orange-50 border border-orange-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-3">Certifications to confirm</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>CPR and First Aid</li>
      <li>Newborn care training</li>
      <li>Optional but helpful: CNA, postpartum doula, lactation education</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

### Good interview questions

<div class="bg-slate-50 rounded-lg p-4 text-sm my-6">
  <ul class="space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>How many families have you worked with?</li>
    <li>Have you supported C-section recovery or twins?</li>
    <li>What was the most difficult case you handled?</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## 2. Communication Skills

<div class="bg-white rounded-xl border border-slate-200 p-5 my-6">
  <p class="text-sm mb-3">A helper can be very experienced and still be the wrong fit if communication is poor.</p>
  <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
    <li>Does she listen carefully and answer directly?</li>
    <li>Can she explain what she is doing and why?</li>
    <li>Does she respect your parenting preferences and household boundaries?</li>
    <li>In the U.S., can she handle basic English communication if needed?</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## 3. Professionalism and Practical Knowledge

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-3 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-t-4 border-orange-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-700 mb-3">Newborn Care</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Feeding and burping</li>
      <li>Diapering and rash prevention</li>
      <li>Bathing and umbilical care</li>
      <li>Recognizing normal vs concerning signs</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-t-4 border-teal-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-teal-700 mb-3">Maternal Recovery</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Basic vaginal birth and C-section recovery differences</li>
      <li>Lochia and swelling awareness</li>
      <li>Rest support and routine monitoring</li>
      <li>Awareness of postpartum mood concerns</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm p-5 border-t-4 border-blue-400">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-700 mb-3">Breastfeeding Support</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Latch basics</li>
      <li>Positioning help</li>
      <li>Engorgement awareness</li>
      <li>Knowing when to refer to a professional</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 4. Personality and Attitude

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-green-700 mb-3">Strong signs</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Calm with babies and parents</li>
      <li>Warm, clean, and reliable</li>
      <li>Admits mistakes and communicates early</li>
      <li>Protects privacy and household boundaries</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-red-50 border border-red-200 rounded-lg p-5">
    <h4 class="font-bold text-red-700 mb-3">Red flags</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Too controlling or dismissive</li>
      <li>Talks negatively about past families</li>
      <li>Too passive or uninterested</li>
      <li>Overly intrusive into personal family matters</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 5. Service Scope and Contract Terms

<div class="space-y-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-xl border border-slate-200 overflow-hidden">
    <div class="bg-orange-500 px-5 py-3 text-white font-bold text-sm">Clarify the exact scope</div>
    <div class="p-5 text-sm">
      <p>Confirm what is included: newborn care, maternal meals, light cleaning, baby laundry, night support, errands, and what is not included.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-xl border border-slate-200 overflow-hidden">
    <div class="bg-teal-600 px-5 py-3 text-white font-bold text-sm">Confirm work conditions</div>
    <div class="p-5 text-sm">
      <p>Write down work hours, days off, sleep/rest time, overnight expectations, meal arrangements, and whether she is live-in or commuting.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-xl border border-slate-200 overflow-hidden">
    <div class="bg-blue-500 px-5 py-3 text-white font-bold text-sm">Use a written contract</div>
    <div class="p-5 text-sm">
      <p>A proper contract should include rate, payment schedule, extra fees, termination rules, refund terms, and confidentiality expectations.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

## 6. Cost, References, and Identity Verification

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg border border-slate-200 p-5 text-sm">
    <h4 class="font-bold mb-2">Before you pay</h4>
    <ul class="space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Ask for a clear rate structure</li>
      <li>Check transportation, holiday, and overtime costs</li>
      <li>Be careful with unusually cheap pricing</li>
      <li>Do not accept vague cash-only arrangements without documentation</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-white rounded-lg border border-slate-200 p-5 text-sm">
    <h4 class="font-bold mb-2">Before you hire</h4>
    <ul class="space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Verify ID and legal work status</li>
      <li>Check 2 to 3 references directly</li>
      <li>Confirm background check status</li>
      <li>Ask about vaccinations and recent health screening</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

## 7. Trial Period and Final Decision

<div class="bg-amber-50 border border-amber-200 rounded-lg p-5 my-6">
  <p class="text-sm mb-2"><strong>Best practice:</strong> start with a short trial period if possible.</p>
  <p class="text-sm mb-0">A 3 to 7 day trial often reveals more than a long interview. Watch how she handles the baby, reports issues, respects your space, and fits your family rhythm.</p>
</div>

### Final checklist

<div class="grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 my-6">
  <div class="bg-slate-50 rounded-lg p-4 text-sm">
    <strong>Must-have basics</strong>
    <ul class="mt-2 space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Relevant experience</li>
      <li>Good references</li>
      <li>Clear communication</li>
      <li>Written agreement</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-slate-50 rounded-lg p-4 text-sm">
    <strong>Trust check</strong>
    <ul class="mt-2 space-y-1 mb-0">
      <li>Do you feel comfortable with her?</li>
      <li>Would you trust her with your baby?</li>
      <li>Does your home feel calmer or more stressed around her?</li>
      <li>Does your instinct say yes?</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

Choosing a postpartum helper is not only a staffing decision. It is a recovery decision. If you screen carefully at the beginning, you lower stress, protect your home environment, and give yourself a much better chance at a stable postpartum period.
